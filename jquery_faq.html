Anacostia Park

  • Dawn and dusk are the best times for wildlife sightings
  • Home to osprey, bald eagles, songbirds, and waterfowl
  • An abundance of plant life thrives alongside the Anacostia River

Fort Dupont

  • Scattered with a wide variety of trees, shrubs and other plants
  • Some of the animals that might be found while walking the trails
    are Whitetail deer, Possums, Black Snakes, Eastern Box turtle,
    Wild turkeys, Pileated Woodpecker, and Red-shouldered Hawks
  • In late spring (May-June) Mountain Laurels are in full bloom
    and perfect time to get out and see the plants that are waking from their winter slumber

Natchez Trace Parkway

  • 52,000 acres are maintained in a "natural" condition, i.e., forests, non-agricultural fields, and open water
  • Over its length it crosses four ecosystem provinces, eight major watersheds, and twelve physiographic regions
  • Home to over 40 different species of reptiles
